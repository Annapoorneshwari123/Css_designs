Sl.no Place Names Type of place Features Information
01 Bangalore Palace Historical Site Inspired by England's Windsor Castle; beautiful architecture and gardens. Built in 1887, it features Tudor-style architecture, ornate woodwork, and an extensive collection of memorabilia.
02 Cubbon Park Park A large green space ideal for walking, jogging, and relaxation. Spanning over 300 acres, it houses various species of flora, statues, and is a hub for morning walkers.
03 Vidhana Soudha Government Building Iconic legislative building; showcases Indo-Saracenic architecture. Completed in 1956, it houses the Karnataka Legislative Assembly and is a symbol of the city.
04 Lalbagh Botanical Garden Botanical Garden Home to diverse plant species and the famous glasshouse; hosts flower shows. Established in 1760, it features a 240-acre garden with a lake, over 1,000 species of flora, and a historical glasshouse.
05 Nandi Hills Hill Station Popular weekend getaway; offers scenic views and historical temples. Located about 60 km from the city, it's known for its sunrise views, trekking paths, and ancient temples like Bhoga Nandeeshwara.
06 ISKCON Temple Religious Site Large and vibrant temple dedicated to Lord Krishna; stunning architecture. Opened in 1997, it is one of the largest ISKCON temples in the world, known for its serene ambiance and cultural activities.
07 Bannerghatta National Park Wildlife Park A biological reserve with a zoo, safari, and butterfly park; great for nature lovers. Established in 1974, it spans over 104 square kilometers and is home to a variety of flora and fauna, including tigers and elephants.
08 Church Street Shopping/Dining Known for cafes, bookstores, and street performances; vibrant atmosphere. A popular hangout spot, it's famous for its art culture, live music, and diverse food offerings.
